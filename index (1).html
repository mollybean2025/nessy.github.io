Nessie: The Loch Ness Monster

The Loch Ness Monster, fondly known as Nessie, is a legendary creature said to inhabit Loch Ness in the Scottish Highlands. This page provides key facts about Nessie, supported by citations, to explore the myth and its cultural significance.

Facts about Nessie

  • Nessie is described in folklore as a large, aquatic creature, often depicted with a long neck and humps, residing in the deep waters of Loch Ness.[1]
  • The earliest recorded sighting of a creature in Loch Ness dates back to 565 AD, when St. Columba reportedly banished a "water beast" in the River Ness.[2]
  • Over 1,100 sightings of Nessie have been documented, fueling ongoing interest and investigations into the creature's existence.[3]
