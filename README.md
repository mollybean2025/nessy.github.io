    <h1>Nessie: The Loch Ness Monster</h1>
    <p>The Loch Ness Monster, fondly known as Nessie, is a legendary creature said to inhabit Loch Ness in the Scottish Highlands. This page provides key facts about Nessie, supported by citations, to explore the myth and its cultural significance.</p>
    
    <h2>Facts about Nessie</h2>
    <ul>
        <li>Nessie is described in folklore as a large, aquatic creature, often depicted with a long neck and humps, residing in the deep waters of Loch Ness.<sup><a href="#cite1">[1]</a></sup></li>
        <li>The earliest recorded sighting of a creature in Loch Ness dates back to 565 AD, when St. Columba reportedly banished a "water beast" in the River Ness.<sup><a href="#cite2">[2]</a></sup></li>
        <li>Over 1,100 sightings of Nessie have been documented, fueling ongoing interest and investigations into the creature's existence.<sup><a href="#cite3">[3]</a></sup></li>
    </ul>
